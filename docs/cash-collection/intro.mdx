# Cash Collection

This documentation explains the complete Cash Collection workflow within the CIT (Cash-in-Transit) Application.

## What is Cash Collection?

In a Cash-in-Transit (CIT) system, **Cash Collection** is a core service feature that bridges the gap between digital banking and physical cash operations. Put simply, it is the process where a client requests a secure, armored CIT crew to visit their physical business location to collect accumulated cash and transport it safely to a secure vault or a bank deposit site.

Instead of clients risking staff safety and financial security by moving large sums of money themselves, they use the CIT Application to request on-demand or scheduled pickups. The application streamlines this entire cycle—from the initial request to real-time route tracking and final secure verification.

## Workflow

1. **Create a Cash Collection:** The client or system triggers a collection request (either as a routine scheduled pickup or a quick ad-hoc request).
2. **Manage the Collection:** Operations teams review, verify, and consolidate collection requests, tracking their status within the system.
3. **Assign the Collection in the CIT Portal:** The collection is assigned to a specific CIT crew, vehicle, and route for secure execution.

:::info
The creation process is identical for both **Scheduled** and **Ad-Hoc** collections once the specific collection type has been selected.
:::
